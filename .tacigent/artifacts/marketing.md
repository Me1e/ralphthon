# Proofline Marketing

Readiness: `needs_validation`

## Launch Objective

Win the first narrow market proof for Proofline by turning live AI buyer-review pain into a repeatable demo and design-partner motion.

Primary near-term objective:

- secure 3 design partners from AI SaaS teams that are actively selling to enterprise buyers

Secondary objective:

- secure 1 fractional security / GRC partner who can bring repeated review workflows

## Build Alignment Report

### Claims retained because build supports them

- Proofline gives teams a review-room workflow for answering AI buyer questions with citations.
- Missing proof can be turned into explicit gap tasks.
- A buyer packet can be published from the review room.
- The local product works end-to-end in production smoke.

### Claims explicitly removed because build does not support them yet

- upload any questionnaire and auto-parse it
- automatically draft answers from a live knowledge base
- support cross-functional collaboration at scale
- replace incumbent trust or GRC systems

## ICP Schema

### Primary ICP

- Company type: AI-native or AI-enabled B2B SaaS
- Stage: seed through Series B
- Team size: 20 to 200 employees
- Selling motion: mid-market or enterprise deals
- Internal state: no dedicated GRC owner, or GRC stretched too thin
- Trigger: first serious AI buyer review lands in an active deal
- Buyer fear: uncited answer slows the deal or creates legal exposure
- Emotional job: get through procurement without sounding sloppy or unsafe
- Functional job: publish a cited answer packet fast enough to preserve deal momentum

### Supporting ICP

- Fractional security / GRC consultants serving multiple startup clients
- Need: one repeatable workspace to turn ad hoc AI review requests into proof packets

## Message Ladder

### Awareness

- Your first AI buyer review is not a compliance project. It is a live deal blocker.

### Interest

- Proofline turns each buyer question into one of three states: cited, needs review, or blocked.

### Desire

- Ship a buyer-ready packet without answering from memory, Slack threads, or uncited docs.

### Action

- Bring one live review. Leave with a packet or a proof-gap list.

## Launch Cells

### Cell 1: Primary

- Segment: Series A/B AI SaaS teams selling to enterprise buyers
- Trigger: procurement, security, or legal review asks AI-specific control questions in a live deal
- Promised outcome: produce a cited packet from one live buyer review without standing up a full trust program
- Proof asset: working demo, passing smoke flow, buyer-packet artifact, screenshots
- Channel: founder-led outbound + warm investor/operator intros
- Success event: prospect agrees to run one live or recent review through Proofline
- Phase: 1
- Priority: high

### Cell 2: Supporting

- Segment: fractional security / GRC consultants serving AI startups
- Trigger: repeated client fire drills around AI diligence questions
- Promised outcome: standardize one review-room workflow across multiple clients
- Proof asset: demo, packet artifact, gap-task workflow
- Channel: consultant outreach + partner referrals
- Success event: consultant brings one startup client into a live teardown
- Phase: 1
- Priority: medium

## Activation Event

Primary activation event:

- a prospect runs one live or recent buyer review through Proofline and reaches a published packet with cited answers in the same working session or within one week

## Time-To-Value Target

- demo time-to-value: under 10 minutes to understand the workflow
- pilot time-to-value: first buyer packet within 48 hours of kickoff

## Success Metrics

- top-of-funnel:
  - 4 live demos from the first 50 targeted accounts
- activation:
  - 3 live review-room walkthroughs that reach the packet stage
- validation:
  - 2 design partners willing to use Proofline on a real buyer review
- commercial signal:
  - at least 1 pilot with explicit budget ownership or consultant-sponsored usage

## Enablement Plan

- founder talk track focused on deal rescue, not compliance transformation
- one sample packet
- three screenshots:
  - queue
  - review room
  - buyer packet
- objection sheet:
  - "Why not Vanta or Drata?"
  - "Why not answer in docs and spreadsheets?"
  - "Why not wait until we hire GRC?"

## Post-Launch Review

Weekly review questions:

1. Did prospects actually bring a live buyer review?
2. Did they care more about proof discipline or automation depth?
3. Was the review-room wedge enough, or was import/export the first hard blocker?
4. Which persona pulled hardest: founder, security lead, or consultant?

## Readiness Rationale

Marketing stays at `needs_validation` because:

- the product proof is real, but customer proof is not yet real
- the category is validated, but Proofline's wedge is still a market hypothesis
- the launch system is credible only if it is used to produce design-partner evidence, not vanity demand
