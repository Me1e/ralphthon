# Marketing Exploration

## Method

Selected launch method: `proof-first launch`

Reason:

- Proofline does not yet have broad market proof.
- The build is strongest when shown as a five-minute deal-rescue workflow.
- The correct GTM move is to sell the first painful use case, not a full trust platform.

## Build Alignment Check

Claims allowed:

- first AI buyer review response workspace
- cited packet generation path
- proof-gap capture through tasks
- local demo proving the workflow

Claims removed before marketing synthesis:

- automatic spreadsheet parsing
- portal ingestion
- knowledge-base sync
- cross-functional collaboration at scale
- broad compliance automation

## Research Frame

Questions:

1. Why is the pain hotter now than one year ago?
2. Which incumbents prove the category is real?
3. Which narrow segment will tolerate an unfinished but sharply useful product?
4. Which channels can reach that segment without expensive brand spend?

## Launch Cell Exploration

### Branch 1: AI support and customer-service startups

- Trigger: enterprise buyer asks how AI-generated customer communication is controlled
- Fit: very high
- Proofline advantage: demo data already mirrors this

### Branch 2: Regulated workflow AI startups

- Trigger: first procurement escalation from banking, healthcare, insurance
- Fit: high
- Risk: sales cycles longer and reference burden higher

### Branch 3: Series A/B founders selling "AI copilots" to larger companies

- Trigger: buyer trust review lands before security/GRC hire exists
- Fit: high
- Channel fit: founder-led outbound and peer intros

### Branch 4: Fractional GRC and security consultants

- Trigger: repeated client fire drills around AI questionnaires
- Fit: medium-high
- Advantage: one consultant can drag multiple clients into the funnel

### Branch 5: Developer-tooling teams with AI features

- Trigger: security review asks about logs, human approval, and model boundaries
- Fit: medium
- Risk: some buyers may still route these questions into existing trust programs

### Branch 6: Broad questionnaire automation replacement

- Trigger: high volume of questionnaires
- Fit: low for current build
- Rejected: current product is not deep enough yet

### Branch 7: Procurement portal integration wedge

- Trigger: portal fatigue
- Fit: low for current build
- Rejected: portal import/export is not implemented

## Synthesis

Primary launch cell:

- Series A/B AI SaaS teams selling into mid-market or enterprise accounts and facing their first real AI buyer review without dedicated GRC staff

Supporting launch cell:

- Fractional security / GRC consultants who repeatedly handle those reviews for startup clients

Dropped alternatives:

- broad questionnaire automation replacement
- procurement portal wedge
- generic security team persona
