# Pitch Exploration

## Audience Profile

Primary readers:

- Bass Ventures lens: market quality, expansion path, return potential
- Sendbird platform lens: product depth, extensibility, operational graph
- engineering leadership lens: architecture credibility, execution quality
- tech-lead lens: real demo quality, reasonable scope, technical taste

## Decision Frame

Decision to make:

- should Proofline remain the product direction worth carrying forward into validation, or should it be replaced by a different idea?

## Ask Contract

Approve a six-week validation sprint with three concrete outputs:

1. 10 customer / operator conversations
2. 3 live buyer-review teardowns
3. the next three build upgrades:
   - questionnaire import
   - persistent domain data
   - multi-user collaboration notes

## Live Refresh Record

- implication: buyer diligence pressure has a hard date, not a vague future

### Signal 2

- Consilium on **13 March 2026** agreed a position to streamline implementation of AI rules
- implication: teams face both enforcement pressure and implementation uncertainty, which usually increases buyer questions rather than decreasing them

### Signal 3

- Vanta, Drata, and Conveyor continue to market AI questionnaire workflows directly
- implication: the category is active and budgets already exist

## Narrative Branches

### Branch 1: VC-first market memo

- strong on why-now and expansion path
- weak on product proof if used alone

### Branch 2: tech-lead demo defense

- strong on build credibility
- weak on market and timing if used alone

### Branch 3: objection-first board memo

- strong on honesty
- risk: too defensive for opening

### Branch 4: product-depth memo

- strong on graph expansion from systems, evidence, questionnaires, packets
- risk: can overstate platform readiness

### Branch 5: pure GTM memo

- strong on launch clarity
- weak on engineering differentiation

### Branch 6: anti-template executive memo

- strong on readability and recommendation force
- must still carry enough evidence

### Branch 7: hybrid recommendation deck

- combines market timing, build proof, and explicit ask
- best fit for the judges

## Synthesis

Chosen method:

- evidence-led recommendation with hybrid artifact

Reason:

- the pitch needs to start with a decision, not a story
- the current product is strong enough for a real recommendation, but not strong enough for a scale ask
- therefore the correct ask is validation, not broad commercialization

## Likely Objections

1. Why does this win against Vanta / Drata / Conveyor?
2. Is the pain big enough, or is it just another security questionnaire niche?
3. Is the build real enough to justify continued work?

## Planned Answers

1. Proofline is narrower and earlier in the workflow than incumbent trust stacks.
2. The wedge sits inside enterprise deal motion, where urgency is tied to revenue rather than general compliance interest.
3. The demo is verified in production smoke and uses a coherent data and auth path rather than mock-only UI.
