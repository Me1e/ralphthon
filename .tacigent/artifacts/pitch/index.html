Problem

The pain is not “compliance” in the abstract. It is a live enterprise deal waiting on uncited AI answers.

The narrow customer is a small AI SaaS team selling into mid-market or enterprise accounts without a dedicated GRC owner. The moment of pain arrives when procurement, security, or legal asks AI-specific questions in an active deal.

  • Slack threads replace a real response process.
  • Answers are assembled from memory and stale docs.
  • Missing proof is discovered late, after the buyer is already waiting.
Working diagnosis

The cost shows up in the deal, not in a dashboard.

The commercial case here is an inference from where the work happens. If the answer packet stalls in an active deal, the team loses time, trust, or both.

  • Revenue risk: the review happens inside a live sales cycle.
  • Process risk: early AI reviews are often handled ad hoc.
  • Tool gap: existing trust tools help later, but many teams still lack a first-response workspace.
Why now: the timing is concrete. The European Commission still points to August 2, 2026 for most AI Act rules, the Council of the EU is still refining implementation, and adjacent vendors are already selling into this behavior.

Product Thesis

Proofline is different because uncited answers cannot quietly ship.

Input

Buyer question

One live AI diligence question enters the desk.

Mapping

System + proof

The team links the question to a system and to evidence, not memory.

Constraint

Gap or citation

Missing proof becomes a gap task instead of a soft guess.

Output

Buyer packet

The answer is published as a cited packet that the buyer can review.

The one-line differentiation: a workspace for AI buyer reviews where answers need proof before they ship.

Objections + Ask

The likely objections are real, and the next six weeks should prove or kill the idea quickly.

VC objection

Incumbents can absorb this.

The defense is not to out-platform them now. It is to prove that the first-response workspace wins before a team commits to a broader trust stack.

SaaS objection

This might be a feature.

If teams mainly want import into an incumbent stack, this should not stand alone. That is why questionnaire import is the first upgrade in the sprint.

Engineering objection

The demo is still early.

Correct. The product proves the workflow today. The next sprint needs to add persistence, collaboration, and import before any bigger readiness claim.

This is why the recommendation is a validation sprint, not a budget-heavy launch.
